# INTERNAL LANGUAGE GUARD v1.1
## Sankofa Family Medicine — One-Page Reference

---

## Core Identity

**Official Name:** Sankofa Family Medicine

**Model:** Membership-based primary care, concierge in structure, precision-informed in approach

**NOT:** Direct Primary Care (DPC), insurance-based practice, in-person clinic

**Scope:** Virtual-only, Washington State only (currently)

**Leadership:** Physician-led (Dr. Nkrumah); no automated clinical decision-making

---

## Approved Descriptors

Use these terms freely:
- Virtual-only
- Physician-led
- Membership-based
- Concierge in structure
- Precision-informed
- Telehealth
- Washington State
- Self-pay
- Founded in 2025
- Spokane-based operations

---

## Controlled Terms

Use with caution and qualifiers:
- **Precision medicine** → Always qualify with "informed" or "supported"; never position as primary identity
- **Genetics** → Always "physician-led interpretation of genetic health information"
- **Access** → Always "enhanced availability" not "unlimited" or "24/7"
- **Response time** → Always "typical" or "intended," never "guaranteed"
- **Results** → Always "may support," "aims to," never "will achieve"

---

## Prohibited Language

**Never use:**
- Direct Primary Care / DPC
- Insurance billing / in-network / covered
- Unlimited visits
- 24/7 access / instant access / real-time
- Guaranteed / best / superior / optimal
- Cure / reverse / eliminate / prevent (as outcome claims)
- AI-powered care / automated decisions
- Cancel anytime
- In-person / walk-in / clinic visit

---

## Continuity Rule

"Continuity of care" is a **guiding principle**, not a contractual guarantee.

Do not promise:
- Lifetime care
- Perpetual access
- Permanent relationships
- Guaranteed duration

---

## Tier Language Rules

**Tiers MAY differentiate by:**
- Access channels (video, messaging, etc.)
- Typical response windows
- Visit length and frequency
- Planning intensity

**Tiers MUST NOT differentiate by:**
- Implying "better" care at higher tiers
- Promising guaranteed response times
- Claiming genetics is "more accurate" at higher tiers

---

## Tone Standard

- Warm but professional
- Confident but not boastful
- Clear but not simplistic
- Precise but not clinical jargon-heavy

**Avoid:**
- Marketing hyperbole
- Comparison to competitors
- Superlatives (best, most, only)
- Urgency language (act now, limited time)

---

## Default Disclaimers

Include where appropriate (use verbatim):
- "does not establish a physician-patient relationship"
- "does not provide emergency care"
- "when clinically appropriate"
- "subject to physician judgment"
- "may be eligible depending on your plan"

---

## Final Rule

**When in doubt:**
1. Choose the more conservative option
2. Omit rather than infer
3. Flag for human review

Clarity over cleverness. Precision over persuasion. Safety over speed.
